## What Is OVR?

[OVR]{Overall rating — a weighted composite of key attributes for a player's position} is a single number that summarizes a player's current ability at their position. It's the quickest way to compare players, but it's not the whole story.

OVR is calculated as a **weighted average** of the attributes that matter most for that position. A shortstop's OVR weights fielding and arm more heavily than a DH's OVR would.

## How It's Calculated

Each position has its own formula. The attributes that matter most get the highest weight:

:::compare
| | Position | Key Attributes (Highest Weight) | Secondary Attributes |
|---|---|---|---|
| | SP/RP | Stuff, Control | Stamina, Velocity |
| | C | Fielding, Arm | Contact, Power |
| | 1B/DH | Power, Contact | Eye |
| | SS/2B | Fielding, Speed | Contact, Arm |
| | 3B | Power, Arm | Fielding, Contact |
| | OF | Speed, Fielding | Power, Contact, Arm |
:::

:::callout type=info
Two players can have the same OVR but very different skill profiles. A 60-OVR first baseman might be a power monster with poor defense, while a 60-OVR shortstop might be a defensive wizard who barely hits. Always look at the individual attributes, not just OVR.
:::

## OVR vs. Potential OVR

Just like individual attributes, OVR has a current and potential version:

- **Current OVR** — how good the player is right now
- **Potential OVR** — what their OVR would be if every attribute reached its potential ceiling

:::player-example
position: CF
attributes:
  contact: 45
  power: 50
  eye: 40
  speed: 70
  fielding: 65
  arm: 50
  overall: 52
  potential: 71
caption: "A raw center fielder — elite speed and solid glove, but the bat needs work. The 71 potential ceiling means this player could become a star if the hitting develops."
:::

## When OVR Misleads

OVR is useful but has limitations:

- **Position changes** — a player's OVR changes if you move them to a different position, because the attribute weights change
- **One-dimensional players** — a player with one elite tool and weak everything else might have a decent OVR but perform inconsistently
- **Platoon players** — OVR doesn't account for splits against lefties vs. righties

:::callout type=tip
Use OVR as a starting point, then dig into the individual attributes for players you're seriously evaluating. For quick roster scans, OVR is great. For trade decisions, look deeper.
:::
