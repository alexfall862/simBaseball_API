## How Ratings Work

Every player attribute in the sim is rated on the **20-80 scouting scale**, the same system used by real MLB scouts. The scale represents how a skill compares to the major league average:

:::compare
| | Rating | Meaning | Rarity |
|---|---|---|---|
| | 80 | Elite | ~1-2 per generation |
| | 70 | Plus-Plus | Top of the league |
| | 60 | Plus | Above average |
| | 50 | Average | Major league average |
| | 40 | Below Average | Fringe starter |
| | 30 | Well Below Average | Backup level |
| | 20 | Poor | Barely professional |
:::

:::callout type=info
A "50" doesn't mean bad — it means major league average, which is already very good. Most starters have a mix of 45-60 ratings with one or two standout tools.
:::

## Reading a Player's Ratings

When you look at a player card, you'll see both **current** and **potential** ratings:

- **Current** — what the player can do right now
- **Potential** — the ceiling they could reach with full development

:::player-example
position: SS
attributes:
  contact: 55
  power: 45
  eye: 60
  speed: 65
  fielding: 50
  arm: 55
  overall: 56
  potential: 68
caption: "A solid shortstop with great speed and plate discipline. His potential of 68 suggests he could become a star if his tools develop."
:::

## What the Numbers Feel Like

:::rating
attribute: Contact
scale: 20-80
example: 70
description: A 70-contact hitter is elite — expect a .290+ batting average with few strikeouts.
:::

:::rating
attribute: Power
scale: 20-80
example: 55
description: A 55-power hitter is slightly above average — expect 18-22 home runs in a full season.
:::

:::rating
attribute: Speed
scale: 20-80
example: 40
description: A 40-speed player is below average on the bases — not a stolen base threat, but not a complete liability.
:::

## The Gap Between Current and Potential

A player with 45 current contact but 65 potential contact is a **project**. He's below average now but could become a plus hitter with development. Young players with big gaps between current and potential are the core of your farm system.

:::callout type=warning
Potential is a ceiling, not a guarantee. A player with 70 potential might only develop to 58 if things don't go well. Development depends on age, playing time, and some randomness.
:::
