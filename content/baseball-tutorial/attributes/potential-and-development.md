## How Players Develop

Player development is one of the most important systems in the sim. Young players improve their skills over time, and your job is to put them in position to develop.

Development happens automatically each week during the season. The key factors are:

- **Age** — younger players develop faster
- **Playing time** — players who play develop more than those on the bench
- **Current vs. Potential gap** — bigger gaps mean more room to grow
- **Randomness** — development isn't perfectly predictable

:::callout type=tip
The single most important thing for player development is playing time. A 22-year-old prospect sitting on your bench develops much slower than one playing every day in the minors.
:::

## Development Curves

Players follow general development patterns based on age:

:::compare
| | Age Range | Development Speed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| | 18-22 | Fastest | Peak development years — these players can make big jumps |
| | 23-26 | Moderate | Still developing, but gains are smaller |
| | 27-30 | Slow | Mostly stable — what you see is what you get |
| | 31+ | Decline | Skills start to erode, especially speed and contact |
:::

:::league filter=College
College players develop faster during their 3-4 year eligibility window. A freshman can make dramatic improvements by junior year, making recruiting for potential especially valuable.
:::

:::league filter=MLB
In the MLB system, players develop through the minor league levels. Promoting a player too early can stall development, but leaving them too long wastes their prime years.
:::

## Reading Potential

When scouting a player, you'll see their potential rating. This represents the **ceiling** — the best they could become under ideal conditions.

:::callout type=warning
Potential is not destiny. A player with 75 potential might peak at 62 if they're unlucky or don't get enough playing time. Conversely, a 55-potential player who develops perfectly will be a solid contributor.
:::

## Development Tips

1. **Play your prospects** — bench time kills development
2. **Don't rush to the majors** — let players develop at appropriate levels
3. **Watch for stalled development** — if a player hasn't improved in a full season, they may be near their real ceiling
4. **Age matters most** — a 20-year-old with 50 potential is more interesting than a 28-year-old with 55 potential

:::detail title="Advanced: Development Probability"
Each week, the engine rolls development checks for each active player. The probability of improvement depends on:

- The gap between current and potential (bigger gap = higher chance)
- The player's age (younger = higher chance)
- Whether the player appeared in games that week (playing = higher chance)

When a check succeeds, the attribute increases by 1 point on the 20-80 scale. Multiple attributes can develop in the same week.
:::
