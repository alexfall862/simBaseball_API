## What Is International Free Agency?

[IFA]{International Free Agent — amateur players signed from outside the US/Canada draft system} is the system for signing amateur players from outside the United States and Canada. These players aren't part of the regular draft — instead, teams compete to sign them using **bonus pool money**.

## How IFA Works

The IFA signing period operates differently from the regular draft:

1. **Bonus pools are allocated** — each team receives a pool of money to spend on international signings
2. **Players become available** — international prospects are listed with scouting information
3. **Teams bid on players** — using a per-player auction system
4. **Signings are completed** — players join your organization at the lowest minor league level

:::callout type=info
IFA is a separate path from the draft. Players in the IFA pool are typically from Latin America, Asia, and other international markets. They tend to be younger (16-18) and rawer than college draft picks, but some have elite potential.
:::

## Bonus Pools

Each team gets a bonus pool based on **inverse standings** — worse teams get bigger pools, similar to draft order:

- **Bottom teams** — largest bonus pools, can pursue top international talent
- **Middle teams** — moderate pools
- **Top teams** — smallest pools, must be selective

:::callout type=warning
Your bonus pool is a hard cap. Once it's gone, you can't sign any more international free agents until the next period. Allocate carefully — don't blow your entire pool on one player unless they're truly elite.
:::

## What IFA Players Look Like

International prospects are typically:

- **Very young** (16-18 years old)
- **Raw but toolsy** — high potential but low current ratings
- **Far from the majors** — 4-6 year development timeline
- **Higher variance** — bigger gap between best and worst outcomes
