## Allocating Your Bonus Pool

Your IFA bonus pool is limited, so how you spend it matters. There are two main approaches:

:::compare
| | Strategy | Approach | Best When |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Star-hunting | Spend most of your pool on 1-2 top prospects | You have a big pool and elite talent is available |
| | Portfolio | Spread your pool across 5-8 solid prospects | Your pool is smaller or the top of the class is weak |
:::

:::callout type=tip
The portfolio approach is generally safer. International scouting is less accurate than domestic scouting, so diversifying your bets across multiple prospects reduces the risk of your entire IFA investment busting.
:::

## Evaluating International Prospects

IFA scouting is harder than domestic scouting:

- **Less data** — fewer games, less reliable competition level
- **More uncertainty** — ratings may be less accurate
- **Physical projection** — 16-year-olds are still growing; tools can change dramatically
- **Signability** — some players have specific bonus demands

## What to Prioritize

When evaluating IFA talent:

1. **Tools over polish** — at 16-17, raw tools matter more than current ability
2. **Athletic profiles** — speed, arm strength, and body type project better than hitting stats
3. **Position value** — SS, C, and CF prospects carry premium value
4. **Realistic ceilings** — a 65-potential player who's more likely to develop is often better than a 75-potential player with major questions

:::callout type=warning
Don't fall in love with one IFA prospect. Even the best international signings have roughly a 30-40% chance of reaching the majors. Spread your investment and expect some misses.
:::

## The 3-Phase Auction

Each IFA signing uses the same 3-phase auction as domestic free agency:

1. **Bidding** — teams submit bonus offers
2. **Negotiation** — the player evaluates offers and may counter
3. **Signing** — the player commits to a team

Higher bids obviously help, but players also consider:

- **Organization development reputation**
- **Clear path to the majors**
- **Bonus pool remaining** (suggests the team is serious about supporting them)

:::detail title="Advanced: Timing your IFA spending"
You don't have to spend your entire pool immediately. The IFA period runs for a defined window, and some teams wait to see how the market develops before spending:

- **Early signing** — locks up top talent before competitors act
- **Mid-period** — players who didn't sign early may lower their demands
- **Late period** — bargain hunting for remaining talent at reduced prices

The risk of waiting is that the best players sign early. The reward is potentially getting better value later.
:::
