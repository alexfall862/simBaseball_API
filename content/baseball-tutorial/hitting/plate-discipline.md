## The Eye Rating

[Eye]{Plate discipline — a hitter's ability to distinguish balls from strikes and lay off bad pitches} is the third critical hitting attribute alongside Contact and Power. It determines how well a hitter recognizes pitches and controls the strike zone.

A high-eye hitter will:

- Draw more walks (higher [BB%]{Walk Rate — percentage of plate appearances that end in a walk})
- See more pitches per at-bat
- Get into favorable counts more often
- Avoid chasing pitches out of the zone

:::rating
attribute: Eye
scale: 20-80
example: 65
description: A 65-eye hitter will draw 10-12% walks and rarely chase outside the zone. Expect an OBP well above their batting average.
:::

## Why Plate Discipline Matters

Walks don't get as much attention as hits, but they are one of the most valuable offensive tools:

- A walk is as good as a single for getting on base
- Patient hitters drive up pitch counts, tiring opposing starters faster
- High-eye hitters are more consistent because walks don't depend on batted ball luck

:::callout type=tip
When evaluating hitters, don't just look at Contact and Power. A player with 50 Contact / 50 Power / 65 Eye is often more valuable than one with 55 Contact / 55 Power / 35 Eye, because the first player gets on base far more often.
:::

## Eye and the Lineup

The best spots for high-eye hitters are:

1. **Leadoff** — getting on base sets the table for the whole lineup
2. **#2 hole** — sees lots of plate appearances, works counts
3. **#5 slot** — can capitalize on runners left by the power hitters above

:::compare
| | High Eye (65+) | Average Eye (45-55) | Low Eye (Below 40) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Walk Rate | 10-14% | 6-9% | 3-5% |
| Chase Rate | Low | Moderate | High |
| Avg Pitch Count | 4.1+ | 3.7-4.0 | 3.3-3.6 |
| OBP Boost | +30-50 pts over AVG | +20-30 pts | +10-15 pts |
:::

## The Free Swinger Problem

Hitters with low Eye ratings are called "free swingers." They swing at almost everything, which means:

- They rarely walk
- They expand the zone, giving pitchers an advantage
- Their production depends entirely on Contact and Power
- They are more vulnerable to good pitchers with plus control

:::callout type=warning
A free swinger with elite Contact can still be productive — they'll hack at everything but make contact. But a free swinger with average Contact will strike out a lot and rarely walk, making them a lineup hole.
:::

:::detail title="Advanced: How Eye interacts with pitcher Control"
The at-bat simulation creates a matchup between the hitter's Eye and the pitcher's Control. When a pitcher throws a ball outside the zone, Eye determines the probability the hitter lays off. When a pitcher throws a borderline strike, Eye determines whether the hitter recognizes it.

A high-Eye hitter vs. a low-Control pitcher is the best offensive matchup — lots of walks and hitter-friendly counts. A low-Eye hitter vs. a high-Control pitcher is the worst — the pitcher paints corners and the hitter can't lay off.
:::
