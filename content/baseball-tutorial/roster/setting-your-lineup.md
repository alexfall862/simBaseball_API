## The Batting Order

Your batting order is one of the most important decisions you make. Each spot in the order has a different role:

:::compare
| | Spot | Role | Ideal Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| | 1st (Leadoff) | Get on base | High OBP, speed |
| | 2nd | Move runners, see pitches | Contact, eye |
| | 3rd | Best overall hitter | High OVR, balanced |
| | 4th (Cleanup) | Drive in runs | Power, contact |
| | 5th | Secondary run producer | Power |
| | 6th-7th | Supporting bats | Mixed |
| | 8th-9th | Weakest bats | Defensive value |
:::

:::callout type=tip
The single most impactful lineup decision is putting your best [OBP]{On-Base Percentage — how often a batter reaches base safely} hitter at leadoff and your best all-around hitter 3rd. Everything else is secondary.
:::

## Setting Defensive Positions

Each position in your lineup also needs a defensive assignment. Key rules:

- Every player must be assigned a position they can play
- Players perform best at their primary position
- Avoid putting weak defenders at premium positions (SS, C, CF)

## Bench Strategy

Your bench should include:

1. **Backup catcher** — always carry one; if your catcher gets hurt mid-game, you need a replacement
2. **Utility infielder** — can fill in at multiple infield spots
3. **Extra outfielder** — pinch-hit or defensive replacement
4. **Pinch hitter** — a strong bat for late-game situations

:::callout type=info
Bench players aren't just insurance. A well-built bench lets you make late-game substitutions — pinch-hitting for weak batters in close games and substituting better defenders to protect a lead.
:::

## Common Lineup Mistakes

- **Batting your best hitter 4th instead of 3rd** — the 3-spot gets more plate appearances over a season
- **Ignoring OBP at leadoff** — a fast player with low OBP is a worse leadoff hitter than a slow player with high OBP
- **Playing a weak defender at SS or C** — the runs you lose on defense outweigh the extra offense
- **Not carrying a backup catcher** — if your catcher gets hurt, you're stuck
