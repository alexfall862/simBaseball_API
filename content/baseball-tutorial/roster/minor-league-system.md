## Farm System Overview

Your [MiLB]{Minor League Baseball — the farm system levels below the majors} system is where future stars are developed. The minor league system has multiple levels, from lowest to highest:

1. **Rookie League** — where most drafted and signed players start
2. **Single-A** — first full-season level
3. **High-A** — more advanced competition
4. **Double-A** — where top prospects face serious competition
5. **Triple-A** — one step below the majors, where near-ready players wait

:::callout type=info
Players progress through these levels as they develop. The engine handles promotions within the minors automatically based on performance and development, but you control when to promote players to the active roster.
:::

## Player Development in the Minors

Minor league players develop their skills over time. The key factors:

- **Playing time** — players who play regularly develop faster
- **League level** — players improve more when appropriately challenged (not too easy, not too hard)
- **Age** — younger players develop faster
- **Potential ceiling** — high-potential players can make bigger gains

:::callout type=tip
Don't rush prospects to the majors too early. A 20-year-old with great potential develops better playing every day in Double-A than sitting on your MLB bench. Bring them up when they're ready to contribute immediately.
:::

## Monitoring Your Farm

Key things to watch in your farm system:

- **Top prospects** — your highest-potential minor leaguers. Track their development progress
- **Ready for promotion** — players whose current ratings suggest they can handle the next level
- **Stalled development** — players who haven't improved despite playing time. They may have hit their real ceiling
- **Rule 5 eligible** — players you need to add to your 40-man roster or risk losing in the Rule 5 draft

:::compare
| | Level | Typical Age | Development Speed | Competition Quality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| | Rookie | 18-20 | Fastest | Lowest |
| | Single-A | 19-22 | Fast | Low |
| | High-A | 20-23 | Moderate | Moderate |
| | Double-A | 21-24 | Moderate | High |
| | Triple-A | 22-27 | Slow | Near-MLB |
:::

:::detail title="Advanced: The 40-man roster crunch"
As prospects develop through the minors, they eventually become eligible for the Rule 5 draft. If you don't add them to your 40-man roster, other teams can draft them. But your 40-man only has 40 spots. This forces difficult decisions:

- Which prospects do you protect?
- Which fringe major leaguers do you DFA to make room?
- Can you trade a prospect you can't protect to get value back?

Managing the 40-man roster is one of the most challenging aspects of being a GM. Plan ahead by tracking when each prospect becomes Rule 5 eligible.
:::
