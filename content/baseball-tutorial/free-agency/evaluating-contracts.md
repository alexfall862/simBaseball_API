## Is This Contract Worth It?

Every contract is a bet on future performance. The key question: **will this player provide value that justifies the cost over the life of the deal?**

## Dollars Per WAR

The simplest framework for evaluating contracts is **dollars per WAR**:

- Calculate the player's expected WAR over the contract length
- Divide total contract value by expected total WAR
- Compare to the market rate for $/WAR

:::callout type=info
The market rate for one win (one WAR) fluctuates based on league economics, but it's a useful benchmark. If you're paying significantly more per win than market rate, you're overpaying.
:::

## Age and Contract Length

The most dangerous contracts are long-term deals for aging players:

:::compare
| | Player Age | Safe Contract Length | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| | 26-28 | Up to 6 years | Low — peak years ahead |
| | 29-31 | Up to 4 years | Moderate — some decline expected |
| | 32-34 | Up to 2 years | High — decline is coming |
| | 35+ | 1 year | Very High — production could fall off a cliff |
:::

:::callout type=warning
The back end of long contracts is almost always dead money. A player who's worth 4 WAR at age 30 might only be worth 1 WAR at age 35, but you're still paying full price. This is how bad rosters happen.
:::

## When to Spend

Free agency is best used to:

- **Fill a specific need** when your roster has a clear hole
- **Add a missing piece** to a contending team (the final starter, a shutdown closer)
- **Short-term deals** for immediate value without long commitment

## When to Be Careful

- **Rebuilding teams** shouldn't spend big on free agents — build through the draft
- **Don't chase stars** for the sake of it — overpaying one player limits your flexibility everywhere else
- **Watch the total payroll** — one big contract can prevent you from making other needed moves

:::detail title="Advanced: Arbitration and pre-FA contracts"
Before reaching free agency, players go through [Arb]{Arbitration — salary negotiation process for players with 3-6 years of service time}. During arb years, salaries increase based on performance but are still below market rate. This is when players are cheapest relative to their production.

Smart GMs maximize their window of cheap, controllable talent (pre-arb and arb years) and supplement with targeted free agency. The worst strategy is paying free agent prices for production you could have gotten from your own system.
:::
