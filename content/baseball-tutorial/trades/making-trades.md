## How Trading Works

Trading lets you swap players with other teams to improve your roster. The sim's trade system works through proposals and AI evaluation.

## Making a Trade Proposal

1. **Select the team** you want to trade with
2. **Choose players to offer** from your roster
3. **Choose players to request** from their roster
4. **Submit the proposal** — the AI GM evaluates and responds

The AI considers trade value, roster needs, and competitive situation when evaluating your offer.

## What Makes a Trade Fair

The AI will accept trades that make sense for both sides. Trades are evaluated on:

- **Player value** — [WAR]{Wins Above Replacement}, current production, and contract
- **Prospect value** — potential ceiling and age for young players
- **Roster fit** — does the player fill a need?
- **Competitive window** — contending teams value current production; rebuilding teams value futures

:::callout type=tip
The best trades are ones where both teams get what they need. A contending team might trade a top prospect for a rental ace — the contender gets a playoff push and the rebuilding team gets a future star.
:::

## Trade Tips

- **Know what teams need** — look at their roster holes before proposing
- **Don't lowball** — the AI remembers bad offers and becomes less receptive
- **Salary matters** — teams need to be able to afford the players they receive
- **Include multiple pieces** — 2-for-1 or 3-for-1 trades are often easier to balance

:::compare
| | Trade Type | When to Do It | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Buy (contending) | When you need one piece to push for playoffs | Trade prospect for veteran ace |
| | Sell (rebuilding) | When your veterans have more value as assets | Trade aging starter for young talent |
| | Salary dump | When you need budget flexibility | Trade expensive player + sweetener for cheap replacement |
| | Swap | When both teams have mismatched needs | Trade SS for SP straight up |
:::

:::callout type=warning
Be careful trading top prospects. They take years to develop and minutes to give away. Only trade high-ceiling prospects when you're getting a proven star in return and you're genuinely in a championship window.
:::
