## Planning Your Class

A successful recruiting class isn't just about getting the best players — it's about getting the right players for your program's needs.

## Assess Your Roster First

Before recruiting, look at your current roster:

- **Where are you graduating seniors?** You'll need replacements
- **What positions are weak?** Target recruits who fill holes
- **How deep is your pitching staff?** Pitching depth is always needed
- **What's your competitive timeline?** If you're rebuilding, recruit for potential; if you're contending, recruit for immediate impact

:::callout type=tip
Always recruit at least 2-3 pitchers in every class. Pitching attrition is high in college baseball due to injuries, eligibility, and players leaving for the draft. You can never have too much pitching depth.
:::

## Balancing the Class

A well-balanced recruiting class typically includes:

:::compare
| | Type | Number | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Impact players | 1-2 | High-potential recruits who could become stars |
| | Solid contributors | 3-4 | Reliable players who start or platoon |
| | Depth pieces | 2-3 | Fill out the roster, provide competition |
| | Pitching | 3-4 | Always need arms |
:::

## Timing Your Offers

Early offers signal strong interest and can lock in recruits before competitors move in. But early offers also carry risk — you're committing money before seeing the full recruit pool.

- **Early offers** — use for your top 1-2 targets to beat the competition
- **Mid-cycle offers** — for solid prospects after you've surveyed the field
- **Late offers** — fill remaining needs with whoever's still available

:::callout type=warning
Don't over-recruit. If you sign more players than you have roster spots, some recruits will be unhappy with their playing time and may transfer. Recruit to fill your needs, not to stockpile talent.
:::

## Development Considerations

College players have limited eligibility (3-4 years). When recruiting:

- **Freshmen with high potential** develop over multiple seasons and give you the most value
- **Junior college transfers** can contribute immediately but only have 2 years of eligibility
- **Balanced profiles** develop more consistently than one-tool players

:::detail title="Advanced: The recruiting calendar"
The recruiting period has defined windows. Early commitments can happen before the main signing period, and late commitments fill remaining needs after the initial period closes. Knowing the calendar helps you plan when to make offers and when to wait for better options.
:::
