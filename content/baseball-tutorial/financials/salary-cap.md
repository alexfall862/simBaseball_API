## How Team Finances Work

Every team operates within a budget. Your financial health determines what moves you can make — from signing free agents to extending your own players.

## Budget Components

- **Revenue** — income from attendance, TV deals, and other sources
- **Payroll** — total salary obligations to your players
- **Luxury Tax** — penalty for exceeding the payroll threshold
- **Available Budget** — what you have left to spend

## The Luxury Tax

There's a soft salary cap via the luxury tax. If your payroll exceeds the threshold:

- You pay a **tax** on every dollar over the threshold
- The tax rate increases for repeat offenders
- Extreme overspending results in draft pick penalties

:::callout type=warning
Occasionally exceeding the luxury tax for a championship push is fine. But consistently running over the threshold eats into your future budget and limits your flexibility. Plan your spending across multiple seasons.
:::

## Revenue Sharing

The sim redistributes revenue from high-earning teams to low-earning teams. This means:

- **Big-market teams** pay into the revenue-sharing pool
- **Small-market teams** receive from the pool
- The system prevents the gap between rich and poor teams from becoming insurmountable

## Budget Strategy

:::compare
| | Team Situation | Budget Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| | Contending, big market | Spend to the cap, supplement with targeted FA signings |
| | Contending, small market | Be efficient — supplement cheap core with value FA deals |
| | Rebuilding, big market | Cut payroll now, save budget for when the core is ready |
| | Rebuilding, small market | Minimize payroll, develop from within, sell veterans |
:::

:::callout type=tip
The most successful long-term strategy is building a core through the draft and supplementing with strategic free agency. Teams that rely purely on buying talent in free agency eventually hit budget ceilings and decline.
:::

:::detail title="Advanced: Multi-year budget planning"
Smart GMs plan their payroll 2-3 years ahead. Consider:

- When do your current contracts expire?
- Which arb-eligible players will get raises?
- When do your top prospects arrive (cheap labor)?
- When is your competitive window?

Aligning your spending with your competitive window is the key to sustained success. Spend when you can win; save when you're building.
:::
