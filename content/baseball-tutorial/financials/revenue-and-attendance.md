## What Drives Revenue

Your team's revenue comes from several sources, and understanding them helps you make better financial decisions.

## Attendance

Attendance is the most visible revenue source. It's affected by:

- **Winning** — winning teams draw more fans
- **Star players** — high-profile players boost attendance
- **Market size** — big-market teams have a higher attendance ceiling
- **Recent success** — consistent winning builds a larger fan base over time

:::callout type=info
Attendance doesn't change overnight. It takes sustained winning (or losing) to significantly shift your fan base. A single great season won't instantly max out your stadium, but several years of contending will steadily grow your audience.
:::

## Revenue Sources

## Winning and the Financial Cycle

Success creates a virtuous cycle:

1. **Winning** → higher attendance → more revenue
2. **More revenue** → higher budget → better players
3. **Better players** → more winning → repeat

But losing creates a vicious cycle too:

1. **Losing** → lower attendance → less revenue
2. **Less revenue** → tighter budget → worse players
3. **Worse players** → more losing → repeat

:::callout type=tip
The key to breaking out of a losing cycle is the draft. Cheap, controllable young talent can make you competitive without requiring a big budget. Once you start winning with a homegrown core, the revenue follows.
:::

:::detail title="Advanced: Market size effects"
Market size creates a structural advantage for some teams. A big-market team generating $150M in revenue has far more flexibility than a small-market team generating $80M. Revenue sharing narrows this gap, but doesn't eliminate it.

Small-market teams succeed by being more efficient — developing talent through the draft, making smart trades, and timing their free agency spending to coincide with a competitive window.
:::
