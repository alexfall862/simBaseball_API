## The Regular Season

:::league filter=MLB
The MLB regular season spans approximately 24 weeks, with teams playing 3-4 games per week. The schedule includes:

- **Division games** — most frequent, highest impact on standings
- **League games** — against teams in your league but not your division
- **Interleague games** — occasional matchups against the other league
:::

:::league filter=College
The college baseball season is shorter, with conference play making up the bulk of the schedule. Key phases include:

- **Non-conference games** — early season, typically against weaker opponents
- **Conference play** — the most important stretch for standings
- **Conference tournament** — end-of-season tournament for conference champion
- **NCAA tournament / CWS** — for qualifying teams
:::

## How Standings Work

Standings determine playoff seeding and draft order. Teams are ranked by:

1. **Win-loss record** — the primary tiebreaker
2. **Division record** — matters for division title tiebreakers
3. **Head-to-head record** — between tied teams

:::callout type=info
Only regular-season games count toward standings. Playoff, all-star, and exhibition games are tracked separately and don't affect your record.
:::

## Key Dates

The season includes several important milestones:

:::compare
| | Event | When | What Happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Opening Day | Week 1 | Regular season begins |
| | All-Star Break | Midseason | Brief pause, all-star event |
| | Trade Deadline | ~Week 18 | Last chance for midseason trades |
| | Regular Season End | ~Week 24 | Standings finalized |
| | Playoffs Begin | After regular season | Bracket starts |
:::

:::callout type=tip
The trade deadline is a critical inflection point. Contending teams need to decide if they're buying (acquiring talent for a playoff push) or standing pat. Rebuilding teams should look to sell their valuable veterans for prospects.
:::
