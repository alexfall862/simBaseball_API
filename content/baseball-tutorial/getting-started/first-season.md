## Step 1: Check Your Roster

The first thing you should do is look at your current roster. Every team starts with a full roster of players, but some lineups may need adjusting.

Look at your starting lineup and make sure your best players are in the right positions. Pay attention to [OVR]{Overall rating — a weighted composite of key attributes for a player's position} ratings — higher is better.

## Step 2: Set Your Lineup

Your batting order matters. Generally:

1. **Leadoff** — high OBP, good speed
2. **2nd** — good contact, can move runners
3. **3rd** — your best overall hitter
4. **4th (Cleanup)** — your best power hitter
5. **5th-6th** — solid run producers
6. **7th-9th** — weaker bats, defensive specialists

:::callout type=tip
Don't overthink it early on. Put your highest-OVR hitters in the middle of the order and adjust from there.
:::

## Step 3: Review Your Gameplan

Your gameplan controls how your team plays automatically during games. Key settings include:

- **Steal frequency** — how often your baserunners attempt steals
- **Bunt tendency** — sacrifice bunts and bunt-for-hit attempts
- **Pitching aggressiveness** — how your pitchers attack the strike zone
- **Bullpen usage** — when relievers enter the game

## Step 4: Let It Play

Once your lineup and gameplan are set, you're ready to go. The sim advances week by week. After each week, check:

- **Game results** — how did your team perform?
- **Standings** — where do you rank in your division?
- **Injuries** — anyone get hurt?
- **Player development** — are your young players improving?

## Step 5: Make Adjustments

As the season goes on, you'll want to:

- Adjust your lineup based on performance and matchups
- Make trades to fill roster holes
- Call up minor leaguers who are ready

:::league filter=MLB
- Claim players off waivers
- Monitor your budget for the trade deadline
:::

:::league filter=College
- Manage player eligibility and playing time
- Prepare for the recruiting period
:::

:::callout type=info
Don't panic if you lose early. A full season is long, and even great teams have losing streaks. Focus on building a consistent roster.
:::
