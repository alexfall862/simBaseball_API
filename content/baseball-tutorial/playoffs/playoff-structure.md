## How Teams Qualify

:::league filter=MLB
The MLB playoff bracket includes the top teams from each league. Qualifying spots:

- **Division winners** — the team with the best record in each division earns an automatic berth
- **Wild card teams** — the best remaining teams by record earn wild card spots

Seeding is based on regular-season record, with division winners receiving the top seeds.
:::

:::league filter=College
The college postseason begins with the conference tournament and progresses to the NCAA tournament. Top-performing teams earn bids to regionals, super regionals, and ultimately the College World Series (CWS).
:::

## Playoff Series Format

:::league filter=MLB
Playoff rounds use a best-of series format:

:::compare
| | Round | Format | Home-Field |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Wild Card | Best-of-3 | Higher seed |
| | Division Series | Best-of-5 | Higher seed |
| | Championship Series | Best-of-7 | Higher seed |
| | World Series | Best-of-7 | Higher regular-season record |
:::
:::

:::league filter=College
The College World Series uses a **double-elimination** bracket format. Teams must lose twice to be eliminated, creating exciting bracket play with elimination games and if-necessary matchups.
:::

## Postseason Differences

Playoff baseball is different from the regular season:

- **Every game matters more** — there's no time to recover from a losing streak
- **Pitching depth is tested** — shorter rest between starts, more bullpen usage
- **Clutch attribute matters more** — high-leverage situations happen every game
- **Stamina management is critical** — you can't rest players like in the regular season

:::callout type=tip
Start managing your pitching staff with the postseason in mind during the final weeks of the regular season. Resting your ace for one regular-season start could mean having them at full stamina for Game 1 of the playoffs.
:::

:::detail title="Advanced: Home-field advantage"
Home-field advantage provides a small but meaningful boost in the sim. The home team gets:

- A slight boost to all player performance
- Last at-bat advantage (batting in the bottom of the inning)
- Favorable park effects for their roster construction

Earning the top seed and home-field advantage throughout the playoffs is a genuine strategic goal, not just a tiebreaker.
:::
