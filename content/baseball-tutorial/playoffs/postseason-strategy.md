## Adjusting for the Playoffs

The regular season is a marathon. The playoffs are a sprint. Your approach should change accordingly.

## Pitching Adjustments

### Shorten Your Rotation

In the regular season, you use 5 starters. In the playoffs, consider using your top 3-4:

- **3-man rotation** — your three best starters pitch on short rest
- **4-man rotation** — slightly more rest, still limits weaker starters
- Skip your 5th starter entirely unless the series goes long

:::callout type=warning
Pitching on short rest is a tradeoff. Your ace on 3 days rest is worse than your ace on 5 days rest, but probably still better than your 5th starter on full rest. Monitor stamina carefully.
:::

### Lean on Your Bullpen

Bullpen usage increases dramatically in the postseason:

- Deploy your closer and setup men earlier if the game is tight
- Use your best reliever in the highest-leverage spot, even if it's the 7th inning
- Carry an extra reliever on the playoff roster if possible

## Lineup Adjustments

### Best Lineup Every Game

No more resting starters. Put your best lineup out there every game:

- Your highest-OVR players should start every game
- Platoon advantages matter less — don't bench a .290 hitter because of a lefty-lefty matchup
- Clutch hitters should bat in the 3-5 spots

### Use Your Bench

Pinch-hitting is more valuable in the playoffs:

- Pinch-hit for weak batters in late-inning high-leverage spots
- Deploy defensive replacements to protect leads
- Have a plan for your bench before the game starts

:::compare
| | Adjustment | Regular Season | Playoffs |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Rotation | 5 starters | 3-4 starters |
| | Closer usage | Save situations only | Any high-leverage spot |
| | Rest days | Regular | Minimal — play to win now |
| | Bench usage | Occasional | Aggressive pinch-hitting |
| | Stamina management | Long-term | Short-term — empty the tank |
:::

:::callout type=tip
The playoffs are when you cash in all the stamina you saved during the regular season. Don't hold anything back. Use your best players as much as possible — there is no next series if you lose this one.
:::

:::detail title="Advanced: Series-level strategy"
Think beyond individual games to the full series:

- **Game 1 matters most** — winning Game 1 in any series gives you a significant advantage
- **Align your ace with high-leverage games** — if you can set up your ace for potential elimination games, do it
- **Bullpen tracking** — a reliever who pitches in Games 1 and 2 may not be available for Game 3
- **Steal signs from the schedule** — know when off days fall in the series and plan your pitching rotation around them
:::
