## How the Draft Works

The amateur draft is your primary way to acquire young talent. Each year, teams take turns selecting players from a pool of amateur prospects.

## Draft Order

Draft order is determined by **reverse standings** — the worst teams pick first. This gives struggling teams a path back to competitiveness through young talent.

:::callout type=info
Unlike some sports, there is no draft lottery in this sim. The team with the worst record gets the #1 pick, period. This means tanking is a viable (if boring) strategy.
:::

## Draft Rounds

The draft has multiple rounds. Key rounds:

- **Round 1** — the best talent. First-round picks are expected to become major league starters
- **Rounds 2-5** — solid prospects with upside. Many future contributors come from here
- **Later rounds** — lottery tickets. Most won't make it, but occasional gems emerge

## The Draft Pool

Draft-eligible players include:

:::league filter=MLB
- **High school seniors** — youngest, highest ceiling, most risk
- **College juniors and seniors** — more polished, lower ceiling, safer
- **Junior college players** — somewhere in between
:::

:::league filter=College
- **High school seniors** — your primary recruiting targets
- **Junior college transfers** — can contribute immediately
:::

## What Happens After the Draft

:::league filter=MLB
Drafted players enter your minor league system. They start at the lowest levels and work their way up through development. First-round picks typically reach the majors in 2-4 years; later picks take longer if they make it at all.
:::
